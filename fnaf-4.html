Five Nights at Freddy's 4 is the fourth and final installment in the Five Nights at Freddy's franchise developed by Scott Cawthon. The game was released in 2015 and marks the end of the main series.



The gameplay of FNAF 4 is different from its predecessors, as the player now controls a child in their own bedroom instead of a security guard in a pizzeria. The game takes place in the protagonist's house, and the player must survive five nights while being haunted by animatronic versions of the main characters from the franchise.



One of the significant changes in FNAF 4 is the addition of sound cues, which are essential to the gameplay. The player must listen carefully to sounds such as footsteps, breathing, and other ambient noises to determine the animatronics' locations and movements.



Another significant change in FNAF 4 is the removal of the traditional camera system, making the gameplay more intense and challenging. The player must instead use a flashlight to search for the animatronics in the dark corners of the room.



The story and lore of FNAF 4 are also more complex and mysterious than previous games. The game introduces a new character, a mysterious animatronic named Nightmare, who is speculated to be the embodiment of the protagonist's fear.



The game also features several hidden minigames, which further expand upon the story and provide clues to the franchise's overall mythology. These minigames are difficult to find and require specific actions to trigger, adding a layer of depth to the game's lore.



The graphics and sound design in FNAF 4 are once again top-notch, with realistic animations and a haunting soundtrack that further immerses the player in the game's eerie atmosphere.



Despite its challenging gameplay and complex story, FNAF 4 received mixed reviews upon its release. Some critics praised the game's innovation and storytelling, while others criticized its difficulty and lack of new gameplay mechanics.



Overall, Five Nights at Freddy's 4 marks the end of the main series and delivers a satisfying conclusion to the franchise. The game's innovative gameplay, complex story, and immersive atmosphere make it a worthy addition to the series, and fans of the franchise will appreciate the game's terrifying and thrilling gameplay.
